Lamborghini

Lamborghini, the iconic Italian luxury sports car manufacturer, was founded in 1963 by Ferruccio Lamborghini in Sant’Agata Bolognese, Italy. Initially, Ferruccio made his fortune in the tractor business with Lamborghini Trattori, but he decided to enter the high-performance car market after a dispute with Enzo Ferrari over the quality of Ferrari’s clutches. Key Milestones in Lamborghini's History:

  • 1963: Lamborghini Automobili was founded, and the Lamborghini 350 GT was introduced as the first production car.
  • 1966: The Lamborghini Miura debuted, revolutionizing sports cars with its mid-engine layout.
  • 1970s: The Lamborghini Countach (1974) set new standards for supercars with its radical wedge-shaped design.
  • 1980s: Lamborghini faced financial struggles and changed ownership multiple times.
  • 1998: The company was acquired by Volkswagen Group and placed under Audi's management, bringing technological advancements and financial stability.
  • 2001-2010s: The Murciélago (2001) and Gallardo (2003) became massive successes.
  • 2010s-Present: The Aventador (2011), Huracán (2014), and Urus SUV (2018) solidified Lamborghini as a leader in luxury performance vehicles.
  • 2023: Lamborghini introduced its first hybrid supercar, the Revuelto, marking the brand's transition towards electrification.
Today, Lamborghini is renowned for its aggressive styling, powerful V10 and V12 engines, and cutting-edge technology, maintaining its status as one of the most desirable supercar brands in the world.
