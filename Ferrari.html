Ferrari

Ferrari is one of the most iconic luxury sports car manufacturers in the world, founded in 1939 by Enzo Ferrari in Maranello, Italy.   • 1947: Ferrari produced its first car, the 125 S, powered by a V12 engine.
  • 1950s-1960s: Ferrari dominated Formula 1 and endurance racing, winning multiple Le Mans and F1 World Championships.
  • 1962: The Ferrari 250 GTO, one of the most valuable classic cars, was launched.
  • 1969: Fiat acquired a 50% stake in Ferrari, providing financial stability.
  • 1980s: The legendary Ferrari F40 (1987) became the first road car to exceed 200 mph.
  • 1990s-2000s: Ferrari continued racing success, with Michael Schumacher securing five consecutive F1 championships (2000-2004).
  • 2015: Ferrari became an independent company, publicly listed on the New York Stock Exchange (NYSE: RACE).
  • 2020s: Ferrari introduced hybrid supercars like the SF90 Stradale and plans to launch its first fully electric vehicle by 2025.
